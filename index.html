--- layout: page title: Neighborhood # Index page --- Neighborhood is a community app focused on creating positive interactions between members of a ‘neighborhood’. Users can create and join `neighborhoods` where they can issue requests or respond to other neighbors. The purpose of Neighborhood is to create a stronger community by making it easier to help or ask for help from your neighbors. Competitor’s such as *Nextdoor* try to enhance the community aspect of neighborhoods by creating a news feed for specific areas. We, on the other hand want to focus on simplicity and encourage face-to-face interactions. Our philosophy is focused on building strong local communities by encouraging mutual help between members (neighbors). That is the reason why our system is so simple. There is no news feed, no friend requests, just a simple request-response system. If you just want to get help moving your furniture, you can simply issue a request and someone will respond. Giving a helping hand is the best way to change from being a stranger to being a Neighbor! There are two norms involved in being a good neighbor: _The first is: Be ready to assist your neighbor at those times when physical proximity is important – either in an emergency, or when the assistance costs little and it would be silly to force the other person to go long distances for it (for example, in the United States, loaning a cup of sugar). The second norm is simple: Don’t be offensive. Good neighbors don’t disturb or offend the people next door (or lower the property value of their dwelling). (p. 136)_ Fischer CS (2004) _The Urban Experience_. 2nd ed. Belmont, CA: Wadsworth/Thomson Learning *...the results of the presented study produced a basic assumption “help needs contact”; this means that asking neighbors for help or providing help to neighbors starts with contacting. Though seemingly basic, it is a critical task that indicates that community social services seeking to build an informal social support network in the neighborhood should start by developing a structure of contacts to bring neighbors together.* [https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fsoc.2019.00046/full](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fsoc.2019.00046/full) (To read) [https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/13607804211012708](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/13607804211012708) Our main competitor is called *Nextdoor*, which is a *US only* application, focused on creating a news feed with local events and posts. While our objectives are the same, our philosophies are completely different. *Neighborhood* is focused on simplicity and encouraging interaction outside the app, in the real-world. We intentionally left out features such as friend-requests, news feeds and other elements that we feel are distracting from our main objective of encouraging cooperation between neighbors. Our content is curated by the administrator of each 'Neighborhood', which also acts as a gatekeeper, deciding who can actually join or not. Having this filter, in the form of the neighborhood admin ensures that the communities being formed will be filled with people that actually have the ability to help each other. Another important difference between *Nextdoor* and *Neighborhood* is the fact that our app will be available worldwide, while *Nextdoor* is only available in the US.
